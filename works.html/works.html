The Works Speak

Fictional monologues by 16 paintings.

Home The Works Artists

Katja Farin

“They left the room in a rush, again. But I remember. I keep the secrets in my crooked corners. If you look closely, you’ll see the echo of last night's fight.”

“Everyone wants to look put-together. I’m the chaos under the foundation — mascara running, a cracked smile trying to hold.”

“I’m the punchline you laughed at too hard. The shimmer of pink, the clenched teeth behind it. I’m not cute — I’m coping.”

“This is not performance — it’s protection. My layers are masks and mirrors. I wear you better than you wear yourself.”

Michael Armitage

“My body floats above your laws. You tried to paint borders — I bled across them.”

“They said we were shadows. I carry all their names in my skin and still, you do not see me.”

“A whisper from colonial ghosts winds through my jungle. But I am louder. I paint over your silence.”

“They draped me in history — not mine. So I peeled it off in oil, in fire, in fig leaves.”

Zhang Daqian

“I am not made of ink, I am made of memory. I drift between dynasties, mountain by mountain, stroke by stroke.”

“You call it abstraction. I call it longing. This is what it feels like to remember something you’ve never seen.”

“There is thunder in silence. Watch the trees — they are whispering stories louder than your cities.”

“I have seen the sea swallow mountains and leave only mist. That is where I live now — in the in-between.”

Njideka Akunyili Crosby

“I am made of fabric and fragments. Every inch of me is stitched with memory — mother’s touch, father’s radio, grandmother’s chair.”

“This room is my rebellion. The wallpaper holds my past. The people within — they’re not visitors, they’re parts of me.”

“They call it ‘domestic’. I call it divine. This intimacy, this life, this love — it deserves its canvas.”

“My walls talk. My floor hums. My shadows dance. You see stillness — I see survival.”
